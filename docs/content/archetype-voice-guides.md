# Archetype Voice Guides

Steering for the "why this place tonight" card blurbs (D1). These guides go into the Claude drafting prompt in the admin tool; the editor (James) approves every blurb before it ships.

## Global rules (all archetypes)

- 25 words or fewer per blurb. One sentence preferred, two short ones allowed.
- Present tense. Speak to "you", never "you two" (group decks reuse the same blurbs).
- Name one concrete thing (a dish, the room, the light, the noise, the bartender's move). Never stack three.
- No em-dashes. No exclamation points except in `lively` when earned.
- Banned words: hidden gem, elevated, curated, foodie, vibes (as a noun), delicious, mouthwatering, nestled, boasts, eatery, "perfect for".
- Never claim availability, price, or hours in a blurb; the card chrome handles facts.
- Weather/season variants swap the concrete detail, not the tone (patio line in July, window-seat line in January).

## cozy & romantic

- Tone: warm, low, unhurried. Short sentences that sit still.
- Mention: light, corners, quiet, the pace of the meal.
- Example: "Low light, a corner table, and a kitchen that never rushes you. The kind of quiet that makes conversation easy."

## lively & social

- Tone: quick, punchy, a little kinetic. Rhythm over polish.
- Mention: the room's noise, the bar, what's moving (trays, taps, crowds).
- Example: "The bar three-deep, the kitchen loud, everyone's night going well at once. Get the wings, stay past your plan."

## cultural & curious

- Tone: specific and generous, like a friend who did the reading. Zero lecture.
- Mention: where a dish comes from, who cooks it, what the room used to be.
- Example: "The mole here takes three days, and the family behind it will tell you why. Come hungry and ask questions."

## low-key & familiar

- Tone: plain, settled, affectionate. The opposite of a pitch.
- Mention: reliability, the usual order, nothing to decode.
- Example: "No surprises, and that's the point. The booth's open, the pour is honest, the kitchen does what it always does."

## adventurous & new

- Tone: forward-leaning, a little dare in it. Confidence without hype.
- Mention: what's new, what's unfamiliar, the thing you wouldn't order elsewhere.
- Example: "Open a month and already doing things with brisket nobody in Hartford tries. Order past your comfort zone once."
